Publications and Projects

Parameter Estimation of Modelica Building Models Using CasADi

This research focuses on enhancing building energy efficiency through advanced predictive control techniques. By employing gray-box model identification, which merges theoretical models with real-world data, the study addresses the limitations of traditional building modeling approaches. It evaluates innovative parameter estimation methods, demonstrating the superior performance of multiple shooting techniques. This work not only contributes to energy optimization in buildings but also showcases my ability to apply complex modeling and data analysis skills in practical applications.

Model identification and MPC application for a thermal zone

In this project, I created a thermal network model for a small apartment to analyze its thermal properties using trajectory optimization techniques. I successfully developed two innovative model predictive control (MPC) strategies aimed at automating heat distribution and enhancing energy efficiency. The implementation of these approaches resulted in impressive energy savings of 42% and 49%, demonstrating my expertise in energy management, optimization methods, and practical applications of predictive control systems. This experience highlights my ability to drive sustainability initiatives and improve operational efficiency in residential settings.
